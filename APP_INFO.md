# App Info

Find information here about the purpose, brand, and target audience of the app.

## Purpose

**One sentence:** Stems is the shareable shopfront for small flower growers, a
single page for what they have in season that they update in minutes and share
with one link.

**One paragraph:** Stems gives small and freelance flower growers (field,
polytunnel, allotment, or back-garden scale, mostly in the UK) one good-looking
page for their live flower availability, with photos, prices, and what's in
season now. They share it with a single link (top of their Instagram, in their
bio, on a market sign) instead of retyping their stock list into a dozen separate
chats. Buyers, mainly local florists and event florists, plus members of the
public who want local seasonal flowers, can also search and browse growers by
name or area without signing in, then contact the grower directly. Stems takes no
cut and never sits between a grower and their buyer. It is, in spirit, the
"Linktree or Bandcamp for a small flower grower": the thing that makes them look
legitimate and findable without building a website. It helps growers advertise
themselves; it is not a faceless marketplace that owns the customer.

The deeper goal is to support the British-grown / "slow flowers" movement with a
practical tool: distributed, local sourcing of seasonal flowers instead of
airfreighted stock from the Dutch auction. This is a relationship-driven,
small-scale world, and Stems is built to fit it, not flatten it.

## Brand / style

- **Name:** Stems.
- **Tagline / descriptor:** "Local-grown flowers, straight from the grower."
  Rhythm line: "Local · Seasonal · Grown."
- **Aesthetic:** Toast × Instagram. Warm, understated, editorial, classy. Pure
  white canvas, a single coral-peach accent (`peach-500 #E87767`, not orange),
  EB Garamond serif for display + Inter for UI. Borderless, content-forward,
  Instagram-narrow column. The flowers bring the colour. (Full detail in
  `DESIGN.md`.)
- **Tone of voice:** Warm, calm, unhurried, quietly confident, honest. A grower's
  friend who happens to write well. Never hypey, corporate, salesy, or punny.
  Celebrates the grower, never patronises. The full voice system lives in
  `marketing/00-foundations/` (master `brand-voice.md`, social `social-voice.md`).
- **Hard rule:** no em-dashes in any user-facing copy (also `.agent/rules/copywriting.md`).

## Target Audience

**Primary (who we acquire): small flower growers.** Freelance, home, and
small-farm growers who sell locally, to florists, at markets, from the gate, for
weddings and events. Time-poor in the season, proud of the craft, already on
Instagram, not especially technical, allergic to corporate or "middleman" vibes.
Often part of or near the British-grown / Flowers from the Farm / slow-flowers
community. **The grower is the hero of the whole product and the marketing.**

**Secondary (the demand the grower brings): buyers.** Local florists and event/
wedding florists sourcing local seasonal stock with a story; and keen members of
the public who'd rather buy just-cut British flowers than imported bunches. In V1
this is demand growers bring with them, not demand Stems generates.

See `marketing/00-foundations/positioning.md` for the full strategic read.

## User Stories

- As a **grower**, I want to claim a handle and set up a profile (farm name,
  area, bio, photo), so that I have a professional page that's mine.
- As a **grower**, I want to list my flowers with variety, colour, price per
  stem, and availability, so that buyers can see exactly what I've got.
- As a **grower**, I want to update availability as I cut and share one link, so
  that I stop retyping my stock list into a dozen chats and people trust it's
  current.
- As a **grower**, I want buyers to contact me directly however I prefer, so that
  I keep my customer relationships and Stems takes no cut.
- As a **buyer**, I want to search for local growers by name or area without
  signing in, so that I can find seasonal flowers near me and reach the grower.
