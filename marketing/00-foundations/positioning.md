# Positioning: what Stems actually is

The strategic heart. Read this before any channel work. Everything else (voice,
channels, content) hangs off the decisions here.

## The one-line read

**Stems is the shopfront for small flower growers.** Not a marketplace people
shop on. A page each grower owns, shares, and updates, the way a small band
or artist uses a single link to show their work when they're too small for a
proper website.

## The honest read on this product

1. The hero is the **grower**, not the buyer. We acquire growers. The florists
   and event buyers come because a grower they already know sent them a link.
2. Stems is not the destination florists wake up wanting to visit. It's the
   thing a grower puts at the top of their Instagram so the people already
   asking "what have you got this week?" can see it without a DM.
3. So we are not selling a marketplace. We are selling a grower a **clean, calm
   place to show what they've grown**, that makes them look as good as the
   flowers do, and saves them re-typing their availability into twelve separate
   chats.
4. The buyer side is real and matters, but in V1 it is **demand the grower
   brings with them**, not demand we generate. Don't build the messaging as if
   florists are our top-of-funnel. They aren't yet.

## The analogy that anchors everything

> Like the page a band or artist makes when they're too small for a website but
> want somewhere to point people. Bandcamp for a bedroom musician. Linktree for
> a maker. A single, good-looking link that says "here's me, here's my work,
> here's how to reach me."

Stems is that, for someone who grows flowers in a field, a polytunnel, an
allotment, or a back garden, and sells them locally. The flowers are the songs.
The availability list is the setlist. The `@handle` is the link in the bio.

This is the most important sentence in the whole marketing plan: **we make
small growers look legitimate without making them build a website.**

## Who the grower is

A real picture, not a persona deck:

- Often runs the growing alongside something else, or as a small main income.
- Sells to local florists, at markets, from the farm gate, for weddings and
  events, or via a weekly bunch/subscription.
- Already on Instagram, posting beautiful photos, fielding "is the dahlia still
  going?" DMs one at a time.
- Time-poor in the season, especially. Cutting at 6am, not building Squarespace
  sites at 11pm.
- Proud of the craft. Cares about seasonality, varieties, doing it properly.
  Part of (or aware of) the British-grown / "slow flowers" movement.
- Not especially technical, and allergic to anything that feels corporate,
  salesy, or like "a platform that takes a cut."

What they want: to be found, to look professional, to stop repeating themselves,
to keep the relationships they already have, and to never feel like they've sold
out to a faceless middleman.

## Who the buyer is (the audience the grower brings)

- **Florists** running a shop or studio who want local, seasonal stock and a
  story to tell their own customers, instead of (or alongside) the Dutch auction.
- **Wedding and event florists** sourcing specific colours/varieties in season.
- **Keen members of the public** who'd rather buy a bucket of just-cut local
  flowers than a cellophane bunch flown across the world.

They value: seasonality, freshness, locality, knowing the person who grew it,
and a supply chain they feel good about. They do **not** want a clunky B2B portal.

## The wedge (how we actually grow)

```
A grower sets up a Stems page (the only thing we have to make happen)
        │
        ▼
They put the @handle link at the top of their Instagram, and in their bio
        │
        ▼
Their existing followers / florist contacts click it to see this week's stock
        │
        ▼
Some of those people are growers too ("how'd you make that page?")  ──┐
        │                                                              │
        ▼                                                              │
Buyers searching "local flowers near me" find growers on /discover     │
        │                                                              │
        ▼                                                              ▼
More growers join because their peers are on it ◀── grower-to-grower word of mouth
```

The loop is **grower-to-grower**. A grower's page is also our best advert *to
other growers*, because the people most likely to admire a grower's setup are
other growers in the same local scene, the same county group, the same
Flowers from the Farm meet-up. That is the channel. See `02-grower-outreach`.

## What we are NOT (say this out loud)

- **Not a faceless middleman.** Stems never gets between a grower and their
  buyer. No bidding, no in-app cut on sales (V1 has no payments at all), no
  "Stems-branded" buyer relationship. The grower keeps the customer.
- **Not the Dutch auction, online.** We are the opposite of that: named people,
  local, seasonal, you-know-who-grew-it.
- **Not a social network.** No follower counts to chase, no feed to doomscroll.
  A calm catalogue, not another thing to feed.
- **Not "Etsy for flowers".** Buyers don't check out on Stems in V1. We're the
  shopfront and the phone number, not the till.

## Why now / why us

- The **British-grown flower movement is real and growing**: Flowers from the
  Farm (a UK co-op of ~1,000+ member growers), British Flowers Week (June),
  the wider "slow flowers" shift away from airfreighted stock. The audience and
  the values already exist. We give that movement a tool, not a sermon.
- Most small growers have **no good way to show live availability**. They've got
  Instagram (great for vibe, terrible for "what's £/stem this week"), a spread-
  sheet they email, or nothing. Stems is the missing piece between the pretty
  photo and the actual order.
- The bar is low and the win is concrete: a grower goes from "DM me for a list"
  to "here's my link" in one evening.

## What success looks like (V1)

The only number that matters first: **growers with a live, shared Stems page.**
Not signups. Not page views. Growers who have actually put their link on their
Instagram and updated their availability at least once. Ten of those, genuinely
using it and telling other growers, is the whole V1 goal.

See `../metrics.md` for the tracking sheet.

## The values we market on (and mean)

Local. Seasonal. Named. Honest. Unhurried. Pro-grower. These aren't slogans to
decorate, they're the actual filter for every decision. If a marketing move
would make a grower feel commodified, rushed, or sold to, it's wrong, even if
it would "convert."
