# 07: AEO / GEO (answer engines)

Getting Stems recommended by AI answer engines (ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini,
Perplexity) when people ask the questions our growers and buyers are asking.

## Why this channel

More and more people ask an AI assistant instead of a search box: "how can I sell
my flowers locally?", "where can I buy British-grown flowers?", "what tools are
there for small flower growers?". When the answer engine names tools and options,
we want Stems to be one of them, described accurately and in our own terms.

Two things make this unusually worth doing for Stems:

- **It compounds.** Being a clear, well-described entity that AI models and their
  retrieval layers understand pays off across every engine, and keeps paying off.
- **Near-zero competition in this niche.** There is no crowded field of "tools
  for small British flower growers" fighting to be the recommended answer. We can
  plausibly become the default mention with modest, honest effort.

## Who we reach

- Growers asking how to sell or show their flowers.
- Buyers asking where to find local, British-grown, seasonal flowers.

## What good looks like

- Asking the four engines our target questions returns Stems, described
  accurately (a shareable page for small growers, no cut on sales, British /
  local). TODO(jamie): run these prompts as a baseline before doing the work, and
  re-run periodically to track movement.
- The description the engines give matches our positioning, not a misread ("a
  marketplace", "an Etsy for flowers" would be wrong).

## How AEO / GEO works for us (two halves)

### On-site (what we control)

- **Answer-first content.** Pages and posts that directly, plainly answer the
  real questions ("how can I sell my flowers locally?"), in clear language a model
  can lift. This overlaps heavily with `../03-seo-content`; good SEO content is
  good AEO content.
- **FAQ schema.** Mark up genuine FAQs (what Stems is, the cut/payments question,
  who it's for, how it works) so both search and answer engines can parse clean
  question/answer pairs. TODO(jamie): coordinate with `../seo-roadmap/` on schema.
- **A clear, consistent entity.** Describe Stems the same way everywhere: the
  shareable shopfront for small British flower growers, no cut on sales in V1,
  growers keep their customers. Consistency across the site, About page,
  directories, and social bios is what lets a model form a stable understanding
  of what Stems is.
- **A strong About / "what is Stems" page** that states plainly what it is, who
  it's for, and what it is not (not a marketplace, not the Dutch auction online,
  no cut). Models lean on these.

### Off-site (where models read about the world)

- **Genuine presence in flower-grower communities.** Mentions and conversations
  in the places the community gathers (see `../02-grower-outreach`,
  `../06-community-press`) are exactly the kind of corroborating signal answer
  engines draw on. Earned, not spammed.
- **Directories and listings.** Get Stems listed accurately in relevant,
  legitimate directories (British-grown / slow-flowers resources, small-business
  and tool directories). TODO(jamie): identify the legitimate directories worth a
  listing; avoid spammy link farms entirely.
- **Consistent description everywhere.** The same plain entity description in
  every listing, profile, and bio.

## Tone and honesty

- Everything here must still be true and in voice. We describe Stems accurately,
  including what it is not. We do not stuff keywords or write for the machine at
  the expense of the human reading it.
- Never gameable tricks. The whole strategy is "be a clear, genuine, well-
  described thing in a niche with little competition", which is durable.
