Jamie & Juliette · the half-acre in Cornwall where it began

It started with a buzzing phone

I grow flowers on half an acre behind the house, and a couple of summers ago I started selling them on the side. The dahlias came in three times a week, the sweet peas were everywhere, and my phone filled up with the same question in a dozen different chats: what have you got this week, and how much?

I was answering each one by hand. On Sunday nights I'd build a spreadsheet of what was ready, email it round, and by Tuesday it was already wrong. The flowers sold themselves — they just needed somewhere to be seen that wasn't buried in a WhatsApp thread.

So we made the thing I wished I had

Jamie builds software, and he got tired of watching me retype the same list every morning. One weekend he made me a single page: everything in season, a price beside each stem, one link to share. I put it at the top of my Instagram and stopped sending spreadsheets that night.

Other growers we knew wanted one too. So we built it properly, for all of us. That's Stems — a good shopfront for the people who grow the flowers, and nothing more complicated than that.

“We didn't set out to build a marketplace. We set out to get the 6am DM pile off my phone.”
