## Build the target list

- [ ] List 30 to 40 candidate growers (aim wide to get 10 to 20 yeses). For each:
      name, handle, region, what they grow, where they sell, why they'd benefit.
- [ ] Prioritise active, proud growers already sharing work with no live-
      availability solution.
- [ ] Start in the founder's own region (easiest to meet in person, warmest).
- [ ] Note which are reachable in person (markets) vs DM vs email.

## Find the gathering places

- [ ] Join / observe the relevant Flowers from the Farm regional group(s).
- [ ] Identify and join active county / regional grower Facebook groups (start
      local). Read each group's rules.
- [ ] Find the next 2 to 3 local growers' / farmers' markets with flower
      stallholders and the dates they run.
- [ ] Identify key voices in the slow-flowers community (growers, educators,
      writers) worth following and genuinely engaging with.

## First, warm outreach

- [ ] Visit a local market and talk to 2 to 3 flower stallholders in person
      (script in README). Offer to show the page on your phone.
- [ ] Send 5 personalised DMs (template in README, personalise every one).
- [ ] Send 5 personalised emails to growers where email is more natural.
- [ ] Offer to set up the page with them in an evening, or just send the link.
- [ ] Log every contact: who, where, channel, date, response.

## Convert and celebrate

- [ ] For each grower who sets up: help them add their first flowers and put the
      link in their Instagram bio (see `../05-email`).
- [ ] Feature each new grower on Instagram and `/discover`, credited and by name,
      as the thank-you.
- [ ] Ask the happy ones, gently, to mention Stems to their FFTF group or grower
      friends (see `../04-word-of-mouth`). Never pushy.

## Around British Flowers Week (June)

- [ ] Plan a light, honest push: feature growers, support the week, an "in season
      for the week" piece. Ride the attention, don't hijack it.

## Ongoing

- [ ] Keep the target list moving: a few personal, personalised approaches a
      week, never a blast.
- [ ] One warm follow-up at most per grower, then leave it.
- [ ] Track growers approached, growers set up, growers who shared their link, in
      `../metrics.md` once it exists.

## Hard rules

- [ ] One grower, one personal message. Never copy-paste blasts.
- [ ] Never post promo into a member group uninvited; be useful first.
- [ ] Lead with their flowers, not our product.
- [ ] If they say no, thank them warmly and move on.
- [ ] No em-dashes, no flower puns, no hype, ever.
