# 06: Community and press

Warm, founder-led PR. Local press, regional radio, gardening and floristry press,
slow-flowers podcasts, and partnerships with the people the community already
trusts.

## Why this channel

The British-grown flower story is genuinely interesting to journalists, radio
producers, and editors, especially local and gardening ones: a named local
grower, the season turning, flowers that haven't been on a plane, a simple tool
helping small growers be found. That is a warm human story, not a tech pitch.

Press and community work also builds the credibility that makes outreach and word
of mouth easier. A grower is far more likely to trust an invite when they've seen
Stems mentioned somewhere they respect.

## Who we reach

- Growers (who read gardening / floristry press and listen to slow-flowers
  podcasts) and the slow-flowers community.
- Buyers and the public (via local press and regional radio) who would rather buy
  local, seasonal flowers.

## What good looks like

- A handful of genuine, warm mentions or features in the right places (a local
  paper, a gardening title, a slow-flowers podcast), each reaching exactly our
  people.
- A real relationship or two with community figures (FFTF organisers, floristry
  educators) who would happily point growers our way.
- Not a press-release blast. Targeted, human, founder-led.

## The angles (all true, all positive-case)

- The named local grower vs the airfreighted, anonymous bunch (positive case,
  never the guilt case, see `brand-voice.md`).
- "A page for your flowers you can share in an evening": a small, practical tool
  for time-poor growers, no cut on sales, growers keep their customers.
- The founder's own why: why build this for British growers now.
- Seasonal hooks: British Flowers Week (June), the start and peak of the season.

## Tone

- Founder-led and personal. No PR-agency boilerplate, no "thrilled to announce".
- Lead with the grower and the story, not the product or any metrics.
- Be honest and small: it's early, it's free to set up, no cut on sales.
- Pitch the journalist a story, not a press release. One real grower beats ten
  adjectives.
