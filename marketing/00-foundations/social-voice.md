# Social voice (Instagram, Reels, Stories, captions)

The voice for **short-form social: Instagram posts, Reels, Stories, and the
captions that go with them.** It's the warmest, most casual register Stems uses,
but it's still Stems: never shouty, never salesy, never punny. For the About
page, blog, marketing pages, and email, see `brand-voice.md`, which is the master
and a calmer register. Where the two overlap, the master wins for long-form
written work; this doc wins for social.

Instagram matters more for Stems than any other channel, because **it's where
the growers already are** and it's where a grower's Stems link lives (top of
profile, bio, Stories). Read `positioning.md`: our job on social is partly to
reach growers directly, and partly to model what a grower's own Stems-linked
posts can look like, so they copy us.

## The voice in one line

A grower posting their own stall, if the grower also had good taste and didn't
overthink it. Quiet, real, a little proud, no performance.

## Core voice rules (do not break)

1. **No em-dashes. Anywhere. Ever.**
2. **No flower puns.** Not in captions, not in hashtags, not ironically.
   "Blooming lovely", "let's grow", "thanks a bunch", "petal to the metal", all
   banned. This is the fastest way to look like every other flower account.
3. **Lowercase-leaning, calm captions.** Short. One or two lines. The photo does
   the work; the caption is the label and the warmth.
4. **No engagement-bait.** No "comment your favourite 👇", no "tag a florist who
   needs this", no "double tap if you agree", no "save this for later!". It's
   beneath the brand and growers can smell it.
5. **Real specifics over adjectives.** "snapdragons, sweet peas, a few late
   ranunculus" beats "gorgeous seasonal blooms". Name the variety, the place,
   the week. Specifics are the whole charm of local flowers.
6. **Celebrate growers, always by name where we can.** Reposting/featuring a
   grower is the best content we have. Credit generously, tag them, link them.
7. **No hype, no urgency tricks.** No "limited spots", no "don't miss out", no
   countdowns. Calm is the brand. If a thing is genuinely time-limited (a
   season, British Flowers Week), state it plainly, that's just true.

## What we post (and why)

Our feed is doing two jobs at once: reaching growers (acquisition) and showing
buyers what local looks like. In rough priority:

1. **Grower features.** A photo (theirs, with permission, credited) + a line
   about who they are and what's in this week + their `@handle` link. This is
   the flywheel: it flatters the featured grower, it shows other growers what
   their own page could be, and it's genuinely lovely content. Most of the feed.
2. **"In season now" posts.** What's coming into British fields this week/month.
   Useful, evergreen-ish, shareable, positions Stems as the seasonal authority.
3. **The why, gently.** Local vs flown-in, the names behind the flowers, the
   British-grown story. The positive case (see master voice), never the guilt
   case. Sparingly.
4. **Show-the-product, softly.** A clean screen-recording of a grower's page, or
   "set yours up in an evening". Rare, and always framed as "look how good your
   work could look", never "sign up now".

## Captions

- One sentence, two at most. Lowercase where it reads naturally. Trail off or
  just stop; no hard sell at the end.
- Name the grower (`@theirhandle`) and the place. Always credit.
- **Hashtags: a few, all real, all lowercase.** The British-flower world has a
  genuine tag ecosystem, use the real ones (#britishflowers #grownnotflown
  #flowersfromthefarm #seasonalflowers #slowflowers + local county/town tags).
  Three to six. No hashtag soup, no generic #love #flowers #instagood.
- **No emoji clutter.** A single functional one is fine (📍 for a location).
  Not 🌸🌿💐✨🙌.

Good caption:

> dahlias, cosmos and the last of the sweet peas this week from @somegrower in
> mid-devon. her stems page updates as she cuts 📍devon
> #britishflowers #grownnotflown #seasonalflowers #devon

Bad caption:

> 🌸✨ BLOOMING gorgeous seasonal blooms from one of our amazing vendors!! 💐
> Who else is LOVING dahlia season?? 👇 Tag a friend who needs these! 🙌
> #flowers #love #instagood #beautiful #nofilter #flowerstagram

Same flowers. One sounds like a grower with taste, one sounds like a brand.

## Stories

- Lo-fi is good. A grower's video of cutting at dawn, reposted with credit, beats
  anything we'd produce. Repost growers constantly (with permission).
- Keep the "link sticker" energy honest: "@somegrower's stock this week" linking
  to their page, not "SIGN UP NOW".
- Polls and questions are fine when genuine ("what's in your garden this week?"),
  not as engagement bait.

## Reels

- Slow, natural, no trending-audio whiplash. Ambient or quiet, characterful music
  only. Never a hype edit with zoom-punches.
- Best Reels are the work: hands cutting, buckets filling, a stall being set up,
  a page being updated on a phone in a polytunnel. Show, don't pitch.
- Captions burned in optional, but keep any on-screen text calm and minimal.
- If a Reel is "about the product", it's a grower using their page in the real
  world, not a feature demo.

## Replying / community

- Reply like a person, warmly, by name. No copy-paste brand replies.
- When growers find us organically ("how do I get one of these pages?"),
  that's gold: answer properly, send them the link, make them feel welcome.
- Never argue the imported-flowers debate in comments. Make the positive case or
  say nothing.

## Word and phrase guide

| Use | Don't use |
|---|---|
| this week, just cut, in season, coming now | "fresh drop", "new arrivals", "restock" |
| @theirhandle, by name, credited | "one of our growers" (un-named, when you could name them) |
| share their link, find them on stems | "shop now", "buy here", "link in bio to PURCHASE" |
| grown not flown, british-grown, local | "eco", "sustainable" (overused), "ethical" (preachy) |
| (calm, full stop, or trail off) | "!!!", "don't miss out", "limited time" |

## The line we never cross

Same as the master: the photos and the story can be as warm and romantic as the
flowers deserve. The product, when it appears, is just a good shopfront a grower
shares with one link. We never imply Stems gets you sales, an audience, or
success. We give growers somewhere lovely to be seen. That's the whole, honest
job, and it's plenty.

## When in doubt

Would the most tasteful grower you can imagine post this from their own account
without cringing? If yes, post it. If it sounds like a brand trying to sound like
a grower, rewrite it or don't post.
